You are likely familiar with the 6-Minute Walk Test and Functional Class assessment. These and other tests help your pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) specialist examine your disease and its impact on you, but did you know your specialist can also combine these individual tests into a fuller picture of your unique PAH?
It’s called your risk status. Your risk status (high, intermediate, or low risk) helps you and your doctor see how your PAH is doing today, and where it could be heading in the future, including your life expectancy (chance of survival over the next 5 years).
The good news is that risk status can change. In fact, improving risk status is now an important goal recommended by experts. Monitoring your risk status at every visit is how PAH specialists today determine whether you’re on the right track, or it’s time to make a change. Then, you can work together to make sure you are on the best path to controlling your PAH.
Together with your PAH specialist, you may find ways to improve your risk status which could be the key to feeling better and living longer. Find out more about what steps you and your healthcare provider can take to try and help improve your risk status.
